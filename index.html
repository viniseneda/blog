  • Sapty hoof hypothetis

    APL programming language originator Kenneth E. Iverson believed that the Sapir–Whorf hypothesis applied to computer languages (without actually mentioning it by name). His Turing award lecture, “Notation as a tool of thought”, was devoted to this theme, arguing that more powerful notations aided thinking about computer algorithms.[79]

    The essays of Paul Graham explore similar themes, such as a conceptual hierarchy of computer languages, with more expressive and succinct languages at the top. Thus, the so-called blub paradox (after a hypothetical programming language of average complexity called Blub) says that anyone preferentially using some particular programming language will know that it is more powerful than some, but not that it is less powerful than others. The reason is that writing in some language means thinking in that language. Hence the paradox, because typically programmers are “satisfied with whatever language they happen to use, because it dictates the way they think about programs”.[80]

    In a 2003 presentation at an open source convention, Yukihiro Matsumoto, creator of the programming language Ruby, said that one of his inspirations for developing the language was the science fiction novel Babel-17, based on the Sapir–Whorf Hypothesis.[81]

(, algo justamente além de qualquer tipo de limite. EXISTEM CONDIÇÔES PARA O CONHECIMENTO HUMANO, EM DIFERENTES NÍVEIS.     Já tinha pensando nisso antes, primeiro em referência a minha memória das diferentes “febres” de pulseiras na minha infância e pré-adolescência. De fazer pulseiras com canos transparentes usados pra fazer instalações elétricas em carros eu acho, que eram ligados com pedacinhos de madeira e preenchidos com água e corante.

    No entanto consigo identificar que essa “moda” está sempre associada a ideia de “coleção” e de “acumulação”. Tanto as pulseiras feitas de cano, quanto as feitas de elástico, as trançadas de fios encerados coloridos e agora as de couro - talvez -, estão sempre aos montes nos braços das pessoas.

    Pensando agora em ocorrências tribais e etc, começo a pensar que a ideia de adornos específicos como pulseiras, tornozeleiras e colares teriam todos uma possível relação com o desenvolvimento e comportamentos humanos mais amplos de alguma forma.
